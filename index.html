About

Kim Dang

Researcher, creative technologist, and maker working across mental health, wearable systems, and fiber art, with a focus on building health technologies that feel both intuitive and human.

I'm passionate about building health wearable systems that are not only intuitive and truly functional, but also beautiful and aligned with what users actually want. My work brings together rigorous research, wearable design, and material practice to make technology feel more human and more usable in everyday life.

M.S. in Information Science, Health Tech concentration, Cornell University.

B.A. in Neuroscience, Boston University.

Published ML Research

Autism Image Classifier

A multimodal imaging project using deep neural networks to study autism-related axon pathology across microscopy, CT, and MRI images.

Served as lead ML researcher, built preprocessing and augmentation pipelines, improved performance by 20%, and helped achieve 91% accuracy across a 7,000-image dataset.
